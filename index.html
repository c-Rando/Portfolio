  1. 1. McDonalds:

    2. I started working at the age of 14. I worked in the kitchen here until I was 17, and felt like I was ready to graduate into more responsibility. This workplace taught me the value of quick, and responsive teamwork. The entire motto of McDonalds is "I'm lovin' it", and you won't be if you have to wait for your food.


  2. 2. Downs Ford Inc.:

    3. Yes, I used to be a salesman. No, they're not ALL bad people. This workplace leaned into many lessons about self-industry. Ford Motor Company REQUIRED us to continue our education, and our jobs depended on it. Here, I learned a wonderful analogy about "alligators in the swamp". You must tackle things that are immediate & urgent before moving to anything else. We must then tackle urgent tasks before moving to immediate tasks. Just because a task is nearby, and ready to be completed, doesn't mean that the task is urgent. If you get frazzled fighting alligators, you'll forget to drain the swamp.


  3. 3. Eastern Fire & Safety:

Little to no training off the bat, I had to evolve and learn what "user-defined-functions", "syntaxes", "scope", etc. concepts were, then actually deliver on those concepts in the field.
